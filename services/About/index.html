Oracle Telecomputing Inc (OTI):

  • Incorporated 1980, sold 2013. Products and capabilities included:
    • Multi-purpose Information Display System (MIDS) for Transport Canada, and then Nav Canada. Provided Aviation Weather, NOTAMs and Flight Planning in all 108 Flight Service Stations (FSS), Flight Information Centres (FIC) and Area Control Centres (ACC) across Canada. A version of MIDS was used on the 2 Canadian NATO Airbases in Germany; and in 410 locations world-wide , for the US DoD. MIDS was based on a triple-redundant IBM PC (IBM XT with 576K of memory and 10 MB hard disk when MIDS was first delivered in 1984). MIDS was recently retired after 30+ years service. Note: MIDS received a special mention by the Auditor General in the mid 1990s as the most cost effective system that Transport Canada had fielded.
    • Flight Information and Billing System (FIBS) for Transport Canada and then Nav Canada. FIBS captured communications to aircraft crossing the North Atlantic that was then billable to airlines/pilots.
    • Gander Automated Message Processing System (GAMPS) for Transport Canada then Nav Canada. GAMPS provided store-and-forward message processing to aircraft crossing the North Atlantic.
    • Suspect/Suspicious Aircraft Alerting System (SAAS) for the RCMP. This system evaluated Flight Plans for suspect aircraft type, ownership, flight routes and flight times. The RCMP said it paid for itself in 1 month.
    • Internet Flight Service (IFS) for Nav Canada. IFS allowed pilot self-briefing (Aviation Weather and NOTAMs) and Flight Plan filing via the Internet.
    • RADAR Display System (RDS) consisting of 2 PAR plus 1 Tower Displays. Used in all 5 Canadian DND Terminal Radar And Control Systems (TRACS) bases.
    • Radar Data Processing System (RDPS) used by DND in Goose Bay (joint Military- Civilian airport) for low-level NATO flight training (Canada, Germany, UK, Netherlands and Italy).
    • RDPS Technology pre-qualified for the $1B+ FAA STARS Program, after an exhaustive technology and Integrated Logistics Support package review.“Ticket” used by the Boeing Team (including OTI). Won by Raytheon.
    • RDPS Technology rated at 97% by UK CAA (norm was 80%); after exhaustive audit; before system was allowed into service in the UK for BAe.
    • Only foreign company to win NASA “Turning Goals into Reality” award for our work in incorporating Dynamic Arrival Spacing into the Center TRACON Automation System (CTAS) as part of Aircraft Vortex Spacing System (AVOSS).
    • Site-adapted prototype NASA CTAS for Toronto. Eliminated 70% of “dead” code.
    • ATM Technology in 20+ countries.
    • Air-Ground Voice Comms Simulator used in 3 countries.
  • Digital Primary Surveillance Radar (PSR) Simulator used by Raytheon (Syracuse) in 8 locations worldwide.
  • Awarded Nav Canada Supplier Of The Year 5 times between 2000 (when it was introduced) and 2013 (when OTI was sold).
